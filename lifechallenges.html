CHALLENGES FACED BY NICHOLAS IN HIS EARLY AGE


Nicholas Kyalo isA student of Moi university

was born in August 2003 in Machakos County, Kathiani sub-location, Mbee village.

His early years were marked by the warmth of his rural surroundings and the love of his family.

However, when Nicholas was in primary school, specifically in Grade 2, his parents' relationship took a turn.

His mother made the difficult decision to divorce his father, leaving Nicholas and his father to face new challenges.

With his mother starting a new life with another partner, Nicholas's father found himself in the role of a single parent, tasked with the responsibility of caring for his son.

The sudden change in family dynamics was not easy, but Nicholas's father did his best to provide for him.

During this time, Nicholas's grandmother stepped in to lend a helping hand.


Teresiah kithei(his grandmother)

She took on the role of raising Nicholas, offering him love, guidance, and stability during this transitional period.

Despite the upheaval in his personal life, Nicholas remained focused on his education.

He continued attending primary school, where his dedication and commitment to learning stood out.

Teachers recall him as a diligent student, always eager to absorb knowledge and excel in his studies.

One particular teacher who made a lasting impact on Nicholas's life was Mr. Mutuku, his English teacher.

Mr. Mutuku's passion for teaching and his ability to inspire his students left a profound impression on Nicholas.

He credits Mr. Mutuku for instilling in him a love for the English language and for shaping his academic journey.

As Nicholas progressed through primary school, he faced various challenges, but his determination never wavered.

However, in 2017, circumstances led to Nicholas having to terminate his primary school education prematurely.

Despite this setback, Nicholas remains grateful for the lessons learned and the support received along the way.

He fondly remembers Mr. Mutuku and expresses his gratitude, saying, "Teacher, be blessed wherever you are. It is you who I am today."

Nicholas's early experiences shaped his resilience and determination, laying the foundation for his future endeavors.

His gratitude towards those who guided him reflects his humility and appreciation for the support that helped him overcome obstacles and pursue his dreams.
